Preview

The Matrix is a science fiction action film released in 1999.
It was written and directed by the Wachowski siblings, and stars Keanu Reeves as Neo, a hacker who discovers that the world he lives in is actually a simulated reality created by sentient machines.
Neo is contacted by a mysterious figure named Morpheus, who believes Neo is "The One," a chosen individual who can defeat the machines and free humanity from their control.
Together with Morpheus and Trinity, another member of the rebellion, Neo sets out to uncover the truth about the Matrix and his role in the fate of mankind.
Along the way, they must evade agents, superhuman beings who can bend the laws of the Matrix to their will.
The film features groundbreaking special effects and action sequences, including the iconic "bullet time" technique.
It also explores themes of free will, fate, and the nature of reality.
The Matrix was a critical and commercial success, and spawned two sequels, as well as various other media.
It has since become a cultural phenomenon and is widely considered one of the greatest science fiction films of all time.
